Charito Murphy has always had an enormous passion for innovation. Innovations that transform fresh ideas into something of value to the community she lives in. From her days as a high school student, she always excel in her class, and with hard work and continued pursuit of excellence, she finished as Class Valedictorian.
With a strong disposition that she could contribute bright innovations in the field of medicine, she took up Bachelor of Science in Pharmacy at the University of Bohol, Philippines. True to her drive for distinction, she capped it off with flying colors.
In her search for opportunities in widening her horizons, she decided to move to Australia to further boost her knowledge and education.
During her early days of traveling back and forth overseas, she envisioned great potential in the building and construction industry. The high-rise edifices that tower the Australian metropolis left her in awe and amazement. As she was excitingly gazing at the building panels that cover the structures, being placed on top of each other, the innovator in her was fuelled which led to a eureka moment. She incepted a revolutionary idea innovating concrete walls that can be a game changer for the building industry.
She carefully designed an interior and exterior decorative concrete walling system that can be easily and quickly mounted and snapped together, creating a smooth, even, and finished surface.
Amidst the criticisms, discouragements, and lack of support from people she is supposed to count on, she continued to develop the idea of contributing something of value to the construction industry, bringing in more employment, and helping ordinary people to have a taste of classy, safe, strong, and affordable home to live in.
In almost 5 years of thorough research, tests, and enhancements, Charito has chosen not to give up despite the challenges that faced her way. Because of her strong determination, and belief that women in the construction industry play a vital part in the economy, she finally invented the first-ever interior and exterior decorative concrete walls, which she calls______________________.
She managed to build houses and apartments in the Philippines through her invention and established a business to have a wider reach to communities and places that wanted to avail of this material. This also facilitated paving for more employment opportunities for a small group of people. Her ultimate goal is to make her creation known around the globe and help more communities in attaining their dreams in building stronger, elegant, yet affordable homes.
